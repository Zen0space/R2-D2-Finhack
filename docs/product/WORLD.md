# The DuitLater World

**A working manifesto · the why beneath the build · TNG FINHACK 2026 · Financial Inclusion Track**

---

## The Problem

A single mother in Felda Gedangsa has TNG eWallet on her phone. Her PayLater allowance is RM 300 — set by TNG based on her financial track record. She needs a sewing machine to start a small home tailoring business. The machine costs RM 1,800.

She has six neighbours in similar situations. Each has a TNG PayLater allowance between RM 200 and RM 400. Individually, none of them can buy the machine. None of them can buy the generator the kampung needs. None of them can afford the bulk school supplies for the new term.

Their **combined** PayLater capacity is RM 2,000. Pooled together, they can buy what each of them cannot. But TNG eWallet has no mechanism to combine PayLater allowances across users. The technology assumes one user = one transaction = one limit.

That assumption is the gap.

---

## The Population

In Malaysia today:

- **B40** households (bottom 40% by income) form roughly 40% of the population — approximately 2.9 million households
- An estimated **15% of adult Malaysians remain unbanked or underbanked** (Bank Negara Financial Inclusion Report 2024)
- Many B40 households **already use TNG eWallet** — 23 million Malaysians carry the rails in their pocket
- Many B40 households **already practice communal financial life** through kutu, family pooling, and informal credit circles
- **Sumbangan Asas Rahmah (SARA)** programme reaches 3.7 million households monthly — direct evidence of who needs help

These are not invisible populations. They are the explicit recipients of national social protection. What they lack is a digital financial product that meets them where they already are.

---

## The Three Existing Pieces

Before DuitLater, three Malaysian institutions already serve this population separately:

1. **TNG eWallet** — universal payment rails. Already in 23 million pockets. Has individual PayLater capacity per user.
2. **NADI (Pusat Sebaran Maklumat Nasional)** — 188 community internet centres run by MCMC nationally · 84 in Selangor alone. Mandate: digital inclusion for B40 + rural communities. Already runs entrepreneurship programmes (NADIpreneur), women-focused digital training (EmpowHER), and provides light banking + courier services in some centres.
3. **MyKasih Foundation** — operates the MySARA programme. Provides cashless aid via MyKad covering 140,000+ basic items in 15 categories at 10,000+ supermarkets and stores nationwide. Established merchant network and item catalogue.

The three institutions have not been integrated into a single product for B40 households. DuitLater is that integration.

---

## The Mission

**Combine TNG PayLater allowances at the household-pool level, deliver MyKasih-eligible items the pool actually needs, and use NADI centres as the on-the-ground facilitator — bringing collective purchasing power to B40 communities without inventing a new payment rail or a new welfare programme.**

Specifically:

1. **Pool capacity = sum of members' PayLater.** No external subsidy stacking. No NGO guarantor. The household's own combined creditworthiness is the cap. Cleaner risk model that matches TNG's existing PayLater approval logic.

2. **AI Penasihat (BM-first) suggests items** from the MyKasih catalogue that match the pool's combined cap, the pool's stated needs, and seasonal context (school start, harvest season, festival period). No black-box scoring — every recommendation cites the price, the category, the reasoning.

3. **NADI centres facilitate.** Pool formation happens at a NADI workstation with centre staff helping members understand the mechanic. NADI staff verify the pool's stated needs are reasonable. NADI provides the trust anchor — members know NADI before they know DuitLater.

4. **Democratic vote.** The pool votes (simple majority) on the suggested item before purchase commits. No member can be charged unless they voted yes or were absent (in which case their share is excluded and the pool reforms or proceeds without them).

5. **Transparent repayment ledger.** Every member's monthly share is visible to every other member. Payments are append-only. Trust score accumulates at the kampung level — a collectivist incentive aligned to existing communal culture.

6. **NADI dashboard.** NADI staff have a portal showing pool health across the kampung, item delivery status, and aggregate repayment rate. NADI's existing role as community facilitator extends naturally into being the kampung's DuitLater steward.

---

## What DuitLater Is Not

- **Not a bank.** No new credit instrument. No interest beyond TNG's existing PayLater rate. We compose existing rails, not invent new ones.
- **Not an NGO programme.** No external charity dependency. NADI is government infrastructure already funded under MCMC's Universal Service Provision mandate.
- **Not a social welfare addition.** MySARA exists separately; DuitLater leverages its merchant network and item catalogue but does not duplicate or compete with it.
- **Not a microfinance lender.** TNG bears the credit risk per individual. We do not introduce additional lending; we orchestrate existing PayLater capacity.
- **Not a credit scorer.** We surface visible kampung-level reliability based on repayment record. We do not predict, score individuals, or judge — we record.

## Test Bed — NADI Felda Gedangsa

**Felda Gedangsa, Hulu Selangor.**

A Felda settlement in northern Selangor, primarily oil-palm and rubber smallholders. Felda settlers (peneroka) have a strong communal management tradition — block-level cooperatives, shared infrastructure, generational continuity. The settlement is served by an existing NADI centre operated under MCMC.

**Why this test bed:**

- **Real Felda + real NADI** — both institutions established, named, and verifiable
- **B40 concentrated** — Felda smallholders are predominantly in the B40 income bracket
- **Strong communal management** — the Felda model is collective by design; pool formation maps onto existing communal structure
- **Geographic accessibility** — ~1.5h drive from FINHACK venue (Bangsar South); site visit feasible
- **Specific stated needs** — agricultural smallholders need cooperative-purchasable items (sprayers, generators, transport, school supplies for children, basic appliances) that exceed individual PayLater limits

**Pilot scope after hackathon (roadmap, not in MVP):** 1 NADI centre · 5 pools · 10 items distributed in first 60 days · evaluate kampung trust score evolution.

- **Tech-stack proportional.** Stack matches scope — Postgres + Hono + Next.js + Alibaba/Qwen optional AI with heuristic fallback. No Kubernetes for a 48-hour MVP.
- **Pitch-ready in 4 minutes.** The story explains itself; the demo proves it.
- **Cultural authenticity.** No imported ROSCA model — Malaysian institutions, in Malaysian voice, on Malaysian rails, for Malaysian households.
