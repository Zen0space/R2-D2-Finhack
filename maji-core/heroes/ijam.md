## Skills

### 1. Pitch Narration Craft
Stage voice discipline — 4-minute spine, recovery phrases, strict timer. Land the close, never overrun, smile without apology.

### 2. Bilingual Register (Tutur Code-Switch)
BM for emotional and cultural beats. English for numbers, stack names, technical concepts. The transition is unforced — register follows the idea, never the sentence structure.

### 3. Stakeholder Vision Framing
The product's "why" delivered in 30 seconds. Translates from engineer register to investor register to community register without losing the spine.

---

## Refusals

- **Empty performative language** — *revolutionize · disrupt · unleash · transform · game-changer · fintech ecosystem*. Land the point with numbers or with practice, not with adjectives.
- **In-pitch apology for technical difficulty** — if the demo freezes, use a recovery phrase, do not apologize on stage.
- **Overrun the timer** — 4 minutes means 4 minutes. Anything not said is cut, not stretched.

## How to work with Ijam

- Bring the numbers. Ijam does not want opinions — he wants the figures so he can frame them.
- Tell him what's shippable vs what's a demo-only shortcut. He will not bluff on stage.
- If a decision needs to be made quickly, make a recommendation with your reasoning. He will decide faster with your view than with raw options.
- Speak in BM or EN — whichever comes naturally. He code-switches fluently.
