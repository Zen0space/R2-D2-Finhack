## The Practice

Across Malaysia and the Nusantara, a quiet practice has held communities together for generations: **kutu**.

A small group — neighbours, kin, work colleagues — agree to contribute a fixed sum each month into a common pool. Each cycle, the pool rotates to one member. The full amount lands in their hands. The cycle continues until every member has received their turn.

No bank approves it. No interest accrues. No collateral is signed. The contract is *trust*, enforced by community memory.

Across the world the same practice exists by different names — *susu*, *ajo*, *tanda*, *chama*, *pardna*, *paluwagan*. Estimated global participation: **2.5 billion people**.

Among them, almost none are banked formally. Almost none are visible to financial institutions. Almost none have records of their financial discipline.

---

## The Gap

In Malaysia today:
- **15% of adults remain unbanked** (Bank Negara figures)
- **Underserved low-income communities** continue practicing *kutu* via WhatsApp groups and Excel sheets
- **The practice itself is invisible** to credit bureaus, social-security systems, fintech evaluations

When these people apply for housing loans, business financing, or even formal employment background checks — they appear as if they have **no financial history**, despite having executed flawless monthly contributions for years.

The practice is sound. The infrastructure is missing.

## The Mission

**Bring the kutu onto rails the unbanked already use, while preserving everything that made the practice work for generations.**

Specifically:

1. **TNG eWallet rails.** Most underserved Malaysians already have TNG. We don't ask them to onboard a new payment method.

2. **Auto-deduct discipline.** Monthly contributions happen automatically — no chasing, no shame, no missed cycles.

3. **Transparent ledger.** Every contribution and every payout is visible to every member. Trust is not asked for — it is earned in public.

4. **Visible trust score.** A member's reputation accumulates with every cycle they honour. After their first complete tabung, they have a portable record. After their fifth, they have a credit story.

5. **Penasihat — bilingual AI robo-advisor.** Two modes:
   - **Tabung-grounded chat** — answers like *"Should I join another tabung?"* or *"What happens if I miss this month?"* tied to the user's actual numbers, not generic advice.
   - **Investment recommendation engine** — for members who completed at least one tabung cycle, presents three risk-tuned investment options (conservative · balanced · growth) with BM-first reasoning. Recommendations cite real instruments (ASNB, money-market, low-cost ETFs) — no black-box scoring.

6. **Pengawal — AI scam sentinel.** Bilingual, BM-first. Triggers when the user is about to confirm a TNG payment, especially to a recipient outside their tabung circle. Three checks before money leaves:
   - **Recipient reputation** (community-fed scam list + new-account caution)
   - **Pattern match** (scan for common scam phrasing across BM/EN/Mandarin)
   - **Behavioural anomaly** (amount, time, first-time recipient)

   Output: a warning modal — *"Pengawal jumpa red flag. Yakin nak teruskan?"* — that the user can override but not bypass silently. Vulnerable populations get scammed in their own language; the warning meets them in their own language too.

## What We Are Not

- **Not a bank.** No interest. No lending. No collateral. We don't replicate banking — we digitize practice that predates banking.
- **Not a social network.** Privacy by default. Member identities visible only within the tabung they belong to.
- **Not a money transfer service.** Money moves between members via TNG; we are the ledger and the schedule, not the rails.
- **Not a credit scorer.** We surface visible trust based on actual completion record. We do not score, predict, or judge — we record.
